NovaFlight Cancellation & Refund Policy (Last updated 2019 — newer rules in draft)

1. Refundable vs Non-Refundable  
   - Refundable fares are eligible for a full refund before departure.  
   - Non-refundable fares:
       • May receive travel credit.  
       • Some regions allow refunds for “significant schedule disruption.”  
       • Policy does not define “significant.”

2. Saver / Basic Economy  
   - Saver fares cannot be refunded or changed.  
   - Exception: Saver may be refunded if purchased with PlusFlex protection.  
   - Exception to exception: PlusFlex is unavailable in some markets.  
   - Flights purchased within 24 hours may be refunded unless booked through third-party channels.

3. Cancellations by Airline  
   - If the airline cancels, customers may be eligible for:  
       • Full refund,  
       • Travel credit, or  
       • Rebooking “at no additional cost.”  
   - Third-party bookings must follow agency rules “unless overridden by mandatory consumer law.”

4. Delays & Interruptions  
   - Delays over 4 hours may qualify for compensation, refunds, or “gesture credits.”  
   - Compensation varies by aircraft type, distance, and ticket class (details pending).

5. Regional Overrides  
   - EU and UK: full refund if canceled, plus compensation based on delay length.  
   - US: refunds only if airline cannot provide “reasonable alternative travel.”  
   - Canada: refunds for delays 3+ hours, but only when controllable by airline.  
   - These rules supersede internal policy except for extraordinary events.
